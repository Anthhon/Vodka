Why Vodka does even exists?

I consider Vodka to be my manifesto against the modern, inflated, and complacent market that has emerged in the field of web development. In today's world, where companies exhibit an increasing urgency to release new web pages, products, and features at an ever-accelerating pace, it has become commonplace to encounter new products or frameworks that function as libraries, shielding developers from truly understanding the intricacies beneath the surface. This is often done in exchange for expediting development and facilitating the implementation of features.

Consequently, a multitude of frameworks, libraries, and methodologies are seemingly "discovered" or released on a monthly basis. While many find these developments convenient, enthusiasts in the realm of computing may find it somewhat disconcerting to sacrifice knowledge and control in favor of such conveniences.

However, merely critiquing the industry and assigning blame without proposing viable solutions or taking concrete action is facile. In response to this predicament, I have developed Vodka as a form of resistance. Vodka is designed to be user-friendly and convenient, yet it refrains from concealing complexities from the developer. All of this is achieved while leveraging the splendid speed and efficiency of the C language.
